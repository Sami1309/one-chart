The Paper

This visualization is based on “All Objects and Some Questions” by Charles H. Lineweaver and Vihan M. Patel, published in the American Journal of Physics 91, 819–825 (2023).

The paper plots every known object in the Universe on a single log-log chart of mass vs. physical radius. Two fundamental boundaries carve out forbidden regions: general relativity forbids objects smaller than their Schwarzschild radius (the black hole line), and quantum mechanics forbids localizing a particle below its Compton wavelength. These boundaries intersect at exactly one point — the Planck-mass instanton, the smallest possible object and a candidate for the initial conditions of the Universe.

Key Physics

The Schwarzschild boundary

rs = 2Gm/c². No object can be compressed below its Schwarzschild radius without becoming a black hole. All black holes lie on this diagonal line (slope +1 in log-log space). The region above it is forbidden by gravity.

The Compton boundary

λc = ℏ/mc. Below this line, quantum uncertainty prevents localization — pair production creates new particles rather than allowing further confinement. Fundamental particles lie on this line.

The Instanton

At the intersection of both boundaries sits the Planck-mass instanton: a black hole whose Compton wavelength equals its Schwarzschild radius. It has Planck mass (~2.18 × 10−5 g) and Planck length (~1.62 × 10−33 cm). It is the smallest possible object.

Isodensity lines

Diagonal lines of slope 3 connect objects of equal density. Life, planets, and stars cluster near atomic density (~1 g/cm³). Neutron stars sit on nuclear density (~1014 g/cm³). The current Universe has critical density ~10−29 g/cm³.

The Creator

Built as an interactive exploration tool to make the extraordinary range of scales in our Universe tangible and navigable. The original paper condenses an astonishing breadth of physics into a single figure — this app aims to let you explore it.

Technical

Built with D3.js and Tailwind CSS. No frameworks, no build step. All physics constants and object data are embedded directly. Wikipedia images are stored locally for instant loading.
